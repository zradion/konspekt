The picture of Dorian Gray

Published in 1890; 82 pages.

characters and places:

Dorian Gray. Certainly wonderfully handsome, with his finely curved scarlet lips, his frank blue eyes, his crisp gold hair.

Basil Hallward - the artist. He and Henry went to Oxford. Has got studio not far from London.

Lord Henry Wotton. Lives on Curson Street. Brown beard, olive-coloured face with worn expression and low languid voice, patent-leather boots, tasselled ebony cane. Smoking innumerable cigarettes.

Lady Brandon. Where Basil have met Dorian.

Lady Agatha - aunt of Henry.

Lord Fermor - a genial if somewhat rough-mannered old bachelor. Uncle of Lord Henry. His father had been ambassador at Madrid, but had retired from the Diplomatic Service in a capricious. He considered that he was fully entitled by reason of his birth, his indolence, the good English of his dispatches and his inordinate passion for pleasure to have offer from Embassy at Paris. The son resigned along with him. And was doing paticulary nothing now.

events:

Studio of Basil Hallward not far from London. He almost finished the portrait of Dorian Gray. Here Basil introduces Lord Henry Wotton with Dorian. Dorian, when saw picture of his self said he would give his soul for he remain young and it grow old.
